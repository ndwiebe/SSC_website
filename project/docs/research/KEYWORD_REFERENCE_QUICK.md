## How to Use This Guide When Writing

### Step 1: Pick Your Anchor Keyword
Example: "GST on Sports Card Sales in Canada"

### Step 2: Use All Variants in the Post
- **Anchor** in title & H1
- **Variants** scattered naturally in body text (first 100 words, H2/H3s, body paragraphs)

### Step 3: Build in Secondary Keywords
- Secondary anchors become separate H2 sections or subsections
- Their variants go in related content

### Step 4: Sprinkle Niche Keywords
- Niche keywords go 1-2 per post naturally (don't force)
- Example: If writing "GST Guide," mention "provincial sales tax" in Ontario/BC section

### Step 5: Internal Link Using Variants
When linking to related posts, use the anchor/variant as link text:
```markdown
[Learn how to value your collection](/blog/how-to-value-sports-cards)
→ Uses variant "value your collection"

[See the hobby vs business test](/blog/hobby-vs-business-cra)
→ Uses primary keyword variant
```

## Tools to Verify Keyword Difficulty

**Before writing a post, check:**

1. **Ahrefs (free tier)** — Search keyword, note: Domain Rating (DR) of top 3 results
   - DR < 30 = Easy to rank
   - DR 30-50 = Medium difficulty
   - DR 50+ = Hard (only for Tier 1 posts)

2. **Google Search** — Look at:
   - Who ranks (are they high-authority sites? blogs?)
   - Are there thin/low-quality posts ranking? (opportunity)
   - Canadian results? (good sign of low competition)

3. **CanIRank** — Free Canadian keyword tool
   - Estimates difficulty with Canada focus
   - Shows opportunities better than US tools

**General rule:** If you don't see established, comprehensive posts ranking, the keyword is low-competition and worth writing.

## Evergreen vs. Trending Keywords

### Evergreen (Write Once, Rank Forever)
```
- how to photograph sports cards
- how to price cards for resale
- adjusted cost base calculation
- gst registration process
- hobby vs business classification

→ Update annually with new data/links, but structure stays the same
```

### Trending (Requires Monitoring)
```
- 2025 market trends
- hot rookie cards 2025
- market recovery 2025
- new grading standards

→ Update monthly, republish with "updated [MONTH]" note
→ Gives search signals that content is fresh
```

**Strategy:** Mix 60% evergreen, 40% trending. Evergreen builds authority; trending captures monthly traffic spikes.

## Red Flags (Don't Write These)

### Too Competitive (Abandon)
```
❌ "best sports cards to buy" (too broad, high-authority sites rank)
❌ "sports card grading" (Beckett, PSA, SGC dominate)
❌ "sports card value" (generic, aggregator sites rank)
❌ "vintage sports cards" (collectors' guides rank, not dealer guides)
```

### Too Niche (Too Much Effort for Traffic)
```
❌ "PSA 10 Gretzky rookie card 1979" (20 searches/month)
❌ "specific card authentication" (10-30 searches/month, niche audience)
```

### Branded (Skip Unless You Own the Brand)
```
❌ "how to use 130point" (130point content ranks, you can't beat it)
❌ "eBay card listing guide" (eBay official ranks, you can't beat it)
```
